Course Description:
This course aims to provide students with key principles and algorithms to build modern Embodied AI systems. Key topics include machine perception, embodied navigation and manipulation algorithms. Through this course, students will learn and practice the foundational principles, techniques, and tools to build new Embodied AI systems.

Target Audience/ Prerequisites: This is a course primarily for junior and senior students.

Course Work:
Grading      Grading will be based on 10 in-class quizzes and attendance (30%), one presentation project (20%), one machine perception homework (20%) and one navigation homework (30%).
In-Class Quizzes
  • The in-class quizzes are designed to complement the lectures and making sure you understand the key knowledge about the course.
  • There are 10 in-class quizzes over the semester. The attendance is taken when you complete the quiz in-class.
  • All quizzes will be 5 multiple-choice questions. You will have 30 minutes (tentative) to finish.
Project 1: Presentation
  • You will select 1 paper from our list of Embodied AI research to present. This should show that you understand the paper, and you could present it to students who do not know the paper well.
  • Can be done in groups of 1-3 people. Score multiplier: 1.1x for solo, 1.05x for two people and 1.0x for three people group.
  • Project 1 is worth 20% credit. You will be graded based on Understanding & Content (40%), Presentation & Communication (30%), Critical Analysis & Discussion (30%), by the teacher, TAs, and peers. Grading rubrics.
Project 2: Machine Perception Homework
  • You will train an object detection model and submit to Kaggle leaderboard.
  • Can be done in groups of 1-3 people. Score multiplier: 1.1x for solo, 1.05x for two people and 1.0x for three people group.
  • Instructions are here.
Project 3: Navigation Homework
  • You will train your own navigation agent in simulation and submit to Eval.ai leaderboard. From the SocialNav benchmark.
  • Can be done in groups of 1-3 people. Score multiplier: 1.1x for solo, 1.05x for two people and 1.0x for three people group.
  • Instructions are here.

Collaboration among Students: We encourage collaboration between students and studying materials in groups when the purpose of this is to facilitate learning, not to circumvent problems. It is allowed to seek help from other students in understanding the material needed to solve a particular problem. However, students must submit individual material and solutions, unless otherwise specified. Students should declare any collaboration on the first page of homework assignments (or equivalently on exercises). If the instructors believe the collaboration is improper, your grade may be affected.

Use of LLM/VLMs (ChatGPT, Gemini, etc.): We encourage you to use ChatGPT to solve the homework assignment problems (because likely in the future everyone will use it for coding). However, you will need to declare using ChatGPT/LLMs in the homework README, as well as the prompts you use. But you are not allowed to use LLM for in-class test!!!
